üîßTroubleshooting

Camera Not Working

  1. Go to Settings > Privacy & Security > Camera
  2. Enable camera access for GhostShot
  3. Restart the app

Photos Not Saving

  • Check that you've selected a category before capturing
  • Ensure sufficient storage space on your device
  • Try restarting the app
  • Check if you're in an active capture session

Photos Disappearing Too Quickly

  • Check your retention settings in each category
  • Remember: photos are automatically deleted based on your retention period
  • Save important photos to your Photos library before deletion
  • Check the recycle bin (24-hour recovery window)

Cannot Access Photos Library

  1. Go to Settings > Privacy & Security > Photos
  2. Set GhostShot to "Add Photos Only" or "Full Access"
  3. Return to the app and try saving again

App Crashes or Freezes

  • Force close the app and reopen it
  • Restart your device
  • Check for iOS updates
  • If issue persists, contact us with details

‚ùìFrequently Asked Questions

How long are photos kept?

Photos are automatically deleted based on your retention settings:

  • 30 minutes
  • 3 hours
  • 1 day (default)
  • 3 days
  • 1 week
  • Delete after sharing

You can set a global default or customize retention per category.

Can I recover deleted photos?

Photos remain in the recycle bin for 24 hours. After that, they are permanently deleted and cannot be recovered. To keep a photo permanently, save it to your Photos library before it's deleted.

Does GhostShot backup my photos?

No. All photos are stored locally on your device and are not backed up to any cloud service. If you want to keep a photo permanently, save it to your Photos library using the save button.

Why doesn't GhostShot save to my Camera Roll?

GhostShot is designed for temporary storage. Photos stay private in the app and won't clutter your Camera Roll. You can manually save any photo to your Camera Roll using the save button in the photo viewer.

Can I sync photos across devices?

No. GhostShot does not support cloud sync. Photos remain on the device where they were captured. This is by design to maintain privacy and security.

How many categories can I create?

You can create up to 5 custom categories (default limit, configurable). Each category can have its own name, icon, color, and retention settings.

Is my data safe?

Yes! All photos are stored in the app's private sandbox on your device. No data is transmitted to any servers. See our Privacy Policy for complete details.

What is Quick Capture Mode?

Quick Capture Mode lets you skip the category selection screen and go straight to the camera. The app will use your last selected category and retention settings. Enable it in the category chooser screen.
